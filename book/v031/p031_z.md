Overall verdict:
The patch successfully repairs the v0.3 weaknesses by converting unenforceable methodological promises into explicit, auditable metadata schemas and generative enumeration rules. It remains strictly debt-bearing and does not promote the artifact.

Does v0.3.1 repair the v0.3 weaknesses?
Yes, structurally. It replaces untyped `Prop` promises with concrete `Bool`-based metadata specifications, explicit admissibility gates, and generative history rules, shifting the burden from "trust the author" to "audit the declaration."

Remaining undefined terms:
`MicroSpace`, `ReversibleUpdate`, `endpoint` (referenced in UnlabeledCount but structurally undefined here), and `CoarseAlternatives` (used in the witness condition but not defined).

Hidden imports still present:
Integer addition (`Int`, `+`) is imported for `SignedTally` without justification (acknowledged as `whySummationDebt`). The concept of "history" implicitly imports a temporal or sequential ordering (acknowledged as `time-smuggling obstruction`). The `Fintype` constraint on `CoarseState` imports decidability and computability assumptions.

Terminology assessment:
The renames successfully reduce overclaim. `ClassicalCount` → `UnlabeledCount` removes the implication of a full classical probability measure. `SignedCount` → `SignedTally` correctly downgrades the operation from a measure to finite bookkeeping. `NonAdditiveWitness` → `SignedTallyCancellationWitness` is a significant improvement, as the mechanism relies on additive integer tallying yielding zero, not on non-additivity. `SignLabel` → `WitnessLabel` removes the quantum-phase connotation.

Label-rule and coarse-graining metadata assessment:
The replacement of `fixed_before_label : Prop` and `generated_by_declared_rule : Prop` with `LabelRuleSpec` and `CoarseGrainingSpec` (using `Bool` flags like `usesTargetOutcome`, `handPainted`, `fixedBeforeWitness`) is a robust structural repair. It prevents implicit hand-painting and result-driven Π by forcing the operator to declare intent in a schema that can be programmatically audited. It cannot prevent bad-faith lying in the metadata, but it prevents ambiguous unenforceable promises.

History enumeration assessment:
The replacement of `histories : List (History M R)` with `HistoryEnumerationSpec` and the mandate that `deduplicate = true` prevents both hand-selected histories and duplicate histories, provided the Go implementation faithfully generates histories from the spec rather than accepting a manual list. The debt on history-selection obstruction is properly acknowledged.

Null-model criteria assessment:
The criteria are strong and comprehensive. The nine specified null models (constant label, endpoint-only, random, hand-painted anti-model, identity Π, constant Π, generic permutation, parity/XOR, cycle-length) cover the primary failure modes of toy signed-bookkeeping systems (trivial counting, overcoarse collapsing, classical parity isomorphism). The explicit downgrade pathways (e.g., `downgrade_to_endpoint_artifact`) prevent false promotion.

Go output schema assessment:
Sufficient and rigorous. The inclusion of `instance_spec`, explicit `null_model_results`, and the `forbidden_inference_audit` block ensures that the Go execution cannot silently drop null-model checks or smuggle physics claims into the output. The schema binds the implementation to the discipline.
