# Rigorous Debt-Aware Framework for Testing Interference-Like Behavior in Minimal Reversible Relational Automata: A Comprehensive EBP v2.1 Research Report

## Executive Summary and Foundational Epistemic Discipline

This report constitutes a rigorous, debt-aware research framework designed to test whether a minimal reversible relational automaton can generate non-additive, interference-like behavior from purely combinatorial rules, without smuggling in quantum mechanical assumptions. The core objective is not to *prove* quantum mechanics, but to develop a formal, auditable methodology that rigorously separates the *derivation* of quantum-like phenomena from their *assumption*. This is achieved through the strict application of the EBP v2.1 (Elephant Behind Physics version 2.1) protocol, a discipline that mandates ideas enter freely but promotion costs explicit, verifiable debt. The framework is built on three foundational pillars: type-level guardrails in Go to prevent accidental category errors; Lean semantic contracts to encode precise mathematical obligations; and EBP v2.1 governance to enforce claim-status transparency and prevent premature overclaiming.

The central artifact of this framework is the `SignedTallyCancellationWitness`, a finite bookkeeping artifact defined as a tuple of an `UnlabeledCount` and a `SignedTally`. Its existence is not evidence of quantum physics, but rather a specific, testable signature of non-additivity within a classical counting regime. To evaluate its significance, the framework subjects the witness to a battery of null-model tests, with the parity/XOR automaton serving as the primary baseline. If the witness is explainable by this simple, classical model, the claim must be downgraded to "classical parity artifact," thereby enforcing epistemic humility [[25](https://stackoverflow.com/questions/2326561/xor-reversibility-operation-question)]. The scope is deliberately confined to a Phase 0.0 toy check, explicitly excluding complex numbers, U(1) phase, Hilbert spaces, and physical interpretations. Any mapping to richer structures is deferred as future debt, ensuring the current analysis remains focused on the logical consequences of the declared combinatorial rules alone [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)].

The report is structured as a comprehensive analytical synthesis, moving from the philosophical and methodological foundations, through the detailed technical architecture of the framework, to a deep examination of its debt structure, potential failure modes, and strategic roadmap. It draws upon a wide array of sources, including formal verification benchmarks for theorem provers like Lean 4 , literature on reversible computing and cellular automata , and foundational work on quantum circuit verification and semantics . The goal is to produce not a final theory, but a robust, living Workbench—a disciplined instrument for exploring the boundary between classical computation and emergent quantum-like structure.

## Methodological Foundations: The EBP v2.1 Protocol and the Goldilocks Principle

The entire research program rests on a bedrock of methodological discipline codified in the EBP v2.1 protocol. This protocol is not a set of arbitrary rules but a carefully engineered epistemic safeguard against the most common pitfalls of speculative theoretical physics: over-interpretation, premature promotion, and the unconscious smuggling of unproven assumptions. Its core tenets are deceptively simple yet profoundly consequential: *ideas enter free; promotion costs debt; debt does not kill; no final-truth claim may be promoted; accounting must not become the work; toy success is not physical proof; Lean/formal proof checks formal consequences, not physical meaning; AI output is unreviewed suggestion* [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. These principles collectively create a "brutal at the throne" environment where the burden of proof is high, and the cost of making a strong claim is made explicit and quantifiable.

The first principle, "ideas enter free," ensures intellectual openness. Prior work, such as the Phase 0a Triality-Winding Kinematic Chain, is imported not as truth, but as a "prior toy artifact / kinematic witness / unpromoted" resource [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. Its value lies in providing a prior formal interface for winding-sector preservation and demonstrating how a contract can be stated without claiming dynamics. However, it is strictly forbidden from entering as proof of physical truth, Bohmian guidance, or triality necessity. This separation allows the Workbench to leverage the accumulated formal machinery while remaining agnostic about its ultimate physical relevance. Similarly, the Phase 0a Downgrade Result is imported as a "downgrade ledger / witness-not-necessity," a crucial record that blocks the automatic use of triality language and motivates the subsequent Phase 0b faithfulness-shadow test [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. This disciplined import ledger prevents the Workbench from becoming a repository of unchallenged dogma.

The second principle, "promotion costs debt," is the engine of accountability. Every time a claim moves beyond a bare statement of possibility, it incurs a specific, named debt. For instance, the claim that a `SignedTallyCancellationWitness` has been generated does not stand alone; it is tethered to debts like `needMap`, which demands a clear definition of how the microstate invariant measure maps to an effective complex information geometry, and `needToyCheck`, which requires the demonstration of actual interference, not just classical probability mixing [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. This transforms abstract philosophical concerns into concrete, actionable engineering tasks. The debt ledger is not a list of failures, but a dynamic map of the path forward, showing exactly what must be paid to advance the idea’s status. It ensures that progress is measured not by the number of claims made, but by the number of debts retired.

The third principle, "toy success is not physical proof," is perhaps the most critical. It establishes a hard boundary between formal consequence and physical reality. A successful toy check in Lean proves only that the formal consequences of the declared axioms hold; it says nothing about whether those axioms correspond to nature [[7](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/300636103_The_Lean_Theorem_Prover_System_Description)]. This distinction is vital because it prevents the conflation of mathematical consistency with empirical validity. The framework is designed to be a "notebook with a conscience," where every conclusion is accompanied by its provenance and its outstanding obligations [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. This directly addresses the "complex smuggling" obstruction, the hidden assumption that "interference-like behavior" can be cleanly generated without secretly relying on complex amplitudes or a 2D real vector space acting as a proxy for $\mathbb{C}$. Discrete reversible systems that mimic interference often implicitly rely on such structures, and this risk must be explicitly monitored as a primary obstruction [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)].

This methodological discipline is operationalized through the Phase 0 Goldilocks Rule, which dictates that the starting substrate must be rich enough to test the target mechanism—phase-like structure, interference-like behavior, probability/current-like accounting, equivariance, and Born-rule-like debt—but poor enough that Hilbert space, complex amplitudes, Schrödinger dynamics, Bohmian mechanics, and Born statistics are not assumed upfront [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. This rule forces a focus on the *minimal necessary structure*, preventing the project from collapsing under the weight of unproven, high-level abstractions. It is a direct response to the historical tendency in physics to start from the most complex, beautiful, or familiar structure (e.g., E₈ gauge groups) and then struggle to reconcile it with observed reality. Instead, the Goldilocks Rule mandates beginning with the simplest possible system capable of expressing the question: "Can non-additivity emerge?" This approach aligns with the philosophy of Einstein's thought experiments, where profound insights arise from stripping away all non-essential complexity to reveal the core structural relationships [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)].

The EBP v2.1 protocol is further reinforced by the requirement for layered, cross-platform guardrails. In Go, distinct types like `UnlabeledCount int` and `SignedTally int` serve as operational guardrails, preventing accidental mixing of concepts that should remain separate, such as signed tally and probability measures [[21](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/393630767_LambdaGo_A_Functional_Extension_of_the_Go_Programming_Language)]. In JSON reporting, string-based naming serves as a dashboard layer, but it is insufficient on its own for EBP discipline because it only prevents overclaim at the presentation level [[11](https://docs.nvidia.com/nemo/guardrails/latest/about/rail-types.html)]. The true enforcement occurs at the type level in Go and the semantic level in Lean. Lean provides the "semantic/proof obligation layer," where the mathematical objects, definitions, and proofs are formally represented, allowing for machine-checked verification of logical consequences . Finally, EBP itself acts as the "claim-status governance layer," providing the overarching rules that dictate how claims are promoted, demoted, or downgraded based on the state of their associated debts [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. This multi-layered defense ensures that category errors are difficult to commit, let alone go unnoticed.

## Technical Architecture: From Lean Semantics to Go Executability

The technical architecture of the framework is a meticulously engineered stack designed to ensure fidelity across different levels of abstraction: from the highest-level semantic intent in Lean, down to the lowest-level executable logic in Go. This stack is not a monolithic system but a series of tightly coupled, yet independently auditable, components, each with a specific role and a clear boundary. The design reflects a deep understanding that formal verification is not about achieving absolute certainty, but about creating a chain of trust where each link can be scrutinized and validated on its own terms.

At the apex of this stack is the Lean 4 theorem prover, which serves as the semantic anchor. Lean 4 is a reimplementation of the Lean interactive theorem prover in Lean itself, addressing many shortcomings of previous versions and offering significant performance improvements . Its power lies in its ability to represent mathematical objects, definitions, and proofs in a single, unified formal language [[7](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/300636103_The_Lean_Theorem_Prover_System_Description)]. In this context, Lean is used to define the core vocabulary and the precise, non-negotiable conditions that constitute a valid `SignedTallyCancellationWitness`. For example, the Lean specification defines `RelState` as a structure containing `raw : Type`, `canonical : Prop`, and `encoding : String`. While `canonical : Prop` is flagged as a placeholder, the intention is to later replace it with a computable function to ensure alignment with the Go implementation . The Lean contract also defines the `HistoryEnumerationSpec`, mandating `deduplicate = true` and generating histories strictly from `allowedStarts` and `maxLength`, thereby closing loopholes for hand-selected or duplicate histories [[6](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2352220823000779)]. This is a critical feature, as it ensures the set of histories is a deterministic product of the declared rules, not an ad hoc list, which is essential for reproducibility and fair null-model comparison [[33](https://openreview.net/pdf?id=wDjOpXKgtU)].

The middle layer is the Go programming language, which serves as the operational engine. Go is a programming language created at Google in late 2007 and released as open source in November 2009, operating as a public project since its inception [[22](https://cacm.acm.org/research/the-go-programming-language-and-environment/)]. Its simplicity, efficiency, and strong tooling make it ideal for building an executable Workbench. The key innovation in the Go layer is the use of **type-level guardrails**, not merely string-based JSON naming. As recommended, Go types like `UnlabeledCount int`, `SignedTally int`, `WitnessStatus string`, and `ForbiddenInferenceAudit struct` are used to create a compile-time barrier against category errors [[21](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/393630767_LambdaGo_A_Functional_Extension_of_the_Go_Programming_Language)]. This means that the Go compiler will raise an error if a `SignedTally` is accidentally passed to a function expecting a `ProbabilityAmplitude`, preventing a class of mistakes that string-based naming alone cannot catch [[30](https://stackoverflow.com/questions/10498547/ensure-a-type-implements-an-interface-at-compile-time-in-go)]. The Go implementation is responsible for computing the canonical encoding of a relational state into a string, a task that must be precisely mirrored in the Lean specification to maintain integrity across the stack [[78](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/369214471_K-Go_An_executable_formal_semantics_of_Go_language_in_K_framework)].

The bottom layer is the JSON output schema, which serves as the human-readable, dashboard-reporting layer. While Go types enforce correctness at compile time, JSON names provide clarity and context for interpretation. The schema includes fields like `unlabeled_count`, `signed_tally`, `witness_status`, and `forbidden_inference_audit`. The `forbidden_inference_audit` object is a masterful EBP safeguard, ensuring that the automated tool cannot accidentally generate overclaimed text (e.g., "quantum interference") even if the numerical tally happens to be zero [[11](https://docs.nvidia.com/nemo/guardrails/latest/about/rail-types.html)]. This layered approach creates a robust system: Lean provides the semantic truth, Go enforces operational correctness, and JSON provides a safe, auditable report. This architecture directly addresses the need for a "formal theorem proving benchmark in Lean 4 for code verification," where models must generate correct, verifiable code that adheres to a formal specification [[33](https://openreview.net/pdf?id=wDjOpXKgtU)].

A critical component of this architecture is the `LabelRuleSpec`, which governs how the `WitnessLabel` is generated. This spec includes boolean flags like `handPainted`, `usesTargetOutcome`, `fixedBeforeWitness`, and `independentlyJustified`. These are not mere suggestions; they are hard, auditable constraints. For instance, `handPainted = false` means the label must be generated by a declared rule, not assigned arbitrarily post-hoc. `usesTargetOutcome = false` means the label generation cannot depend on the outcome of the coarse-graining process, preventing circular reasoning. This metadata is used to compute the `validForWitness` flag, which determines whether the witness is considered valid for the baseline test [[20](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.04755)]. This structure is inspired by modern approaches to semantic typing in smart-contract settings, where static typing is extended to ensure safety for code using dynamically typed constructs [[20](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.04755)]. Similarly, the `CoarseGrainingSpec` includes `fixedBeforeWitness` and `fixedBeforeLabel`, creating a firewall against result-driven tuning of the coarse-graining map $\Pi$ [[19](https://arxiv.org/html/2606.04352v1)]. These specifications transform vague methodological promises into concrete, machine-checkable validity conditions for a witness [[20](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.04755)].

The history enumeration process is another area where the architecture shines. The `HistoryEnumerationSpec` mandates that histories are generated deterministically from a set of `allowedStarts` and a maximum `maxLength`, with `deduplicate = true` enforced. This closes the loophole of manually curating a list of histories that happen to produce the desired cancellation pattern. The histories are enumerated as paths in a graph, where each step is governed by the reversible update rule $U$, ensuring that the collection of histories is a faithful representation of the underlying dynamical system's behavior, not a cherry-picked set [[6](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2352220823000779)]. This is essential for any meaningful test of "interference-like" behavior, as the phenomenon must arise from the dynamics, not from the selection of data points. The framework's commitment to this level of rigor is evident in its treatment of automata theory, where it draws from formal methods for specifying and verifying complex hardware and software systems [[24](https://www.math.pku.edu.cn/teachers/zhangnx/fm/materials/acm-onFM.pdf)].

Finally, the architecture is designed to be extensible and composable. The framework is intended to be used as a companion to other formal verification projects, drawing on diverse repositories covering compiler correctness, programming language semantics, and type theory [[4](https://arxiv.org/html/2602.18307v1)]. This modularity allows the Workbench to integrate best practices from the broader formal methods community, such as the use of timed automata for control system verification [[8](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/228543308_Formal_verification_with_timed_automata_and_devs_models_a_case_study)], or the use of symbolic automata for practical implementation [[70](https://cacm.acm.org/research/automata-modulo-theories/)]. The ultimate goal is not to build a closed system, but to create a disciplined, debt-bearing instrument that can be used to interrogate and refine our understanding of the relationship between classical computation and quantum emergence.

## Core Definitions and Operational Vocabulary: Precision in Terminology

The precision of the framework’s operational vocabulary is not a matter of pedantry but a fundamental requirement for epistemic hygiene. The deliberate renaming of key terms in TC-1A v0.3.1 represents a major terminological upgrade, one that actively prevents conceptual drift and overclaim. The shift from `ClassicalCount` to `UnlabeledCount`, `SignedCount` to `SignedTally`, and `NonAdditiveWitness` to `SignedTallyCancellationWitness` is a decisive move to accurately describe the operation being performed: finite, additive integer bookkeeping over histories, rather than conflating it with probability measures, quantum amplitudes, or norm-squared quantities [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. This is a direct application of the EBP principle that "toy success is not physical proof." By using neutral, descriptive terms, the framework avoids the seductive narrative that a successful tally is evidence of "quantum interference," when it is, at best, evidence of a specific kind of non-additive bookkeeping [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)].

The `SignedTallyCancellationWitness` is the central, bounded artifact of the Phase 0.0 toy check. It is defined as a tuple $(\text{UnlabeledCount}, \text{SignedTally})$, where the former is the count of histories leading to a given coarse state, and the latter is the sum of the `WitnessLabel` values over those same histories. A witness is triggered when the `UnlabeledCount` is greater than zero (indicating multiple paths exist), but the `SignedTally` is zero (indicating a perfect cancellation of positive and negative contributions). This cancellation is the "non-additive" signature—the signal that the system's behavior cannot be explained by a simple, linear combination of its constituent parts. The term "cancellation" is chosen over "interference" to avoid importing the full baggage of quantum field theory, while still capturing the essential mathematical fact of destructive addition [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)].

The `WitnessLabel` is the engine that drives the signed tally. Its definition is intentionally constrained to be a finite, discrete, and non-continuous label. The framework explicitly forbids the use of continuous phase or complex numbers, instead focusing on a `SignLabel` that takes values in the two-element set $\{+1, -1\}$ [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. While the arithmetic of $+1$ and $-1$ is isomorphic to the real part of a complex amplitude, the framework treats this as an open debt (`whySummationDebt`, `originOfSignsDebt`) rather than a solved problem [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. This is a crucial distinction. It acknowledges the mathematical similarity while refusing to grant it physical significance without further justification. The `WitnessLabel` is not a physical quantity; it is a computational bookkeeping device whose origin and purpose must be explicitly declared and justified. The `LabelRuleSpec` enforces this by requiring `generatedByDeclaredRule` and `independentlyJustified`, and forbidding `usesTargetOutcome`, ensuring the label is not a tautological reflection of the very outcome it is supposed to explain [[20](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.04755)].

The `HistoryEnumerationSpec` provides the precise rules for constructing the set of histories that feed into the tally. It specifies the `allowedStarts` (the initial microstates from which enumeration begins), the `maxLength` (the maximum number of steps in a history), and the `deduplicate` flag (which must be `true`). This specification is not an optional feature but a mandatory constraint. It ensures that the histories are not hand-picked but are a deterministic, exhaustive (up to the specified length) exploration of the state space generated by the reversible update rule $U$ [[6](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2352220823000779)]. This is a direct application of the principle that "no literature family automatically solves Phase 0," as the framework refuses to import pre-existing, potentially biased, sets of histories from external sources [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. The enumeration process is a foundational act of the experiment, and its rules must be auditable and reproducible.

The `CoarseGrainingSpec` completes the core vocabulary by defining the map $\Pi$ from the fine-grained microstate to the coarse-grained observable. This spec includes `fixedBeforeWitness`, `fixedBeforeLabel`, and `independentlyJustified`. These flags create a hard audit trail for the coarse-graining process. If `fixedBeforeWitness = false`, it means the coarse-graining map was defined *after* the witness was computed, opening the door to suspicion of result-driven tuning. The framework therefore insists on `fixedBeforeWitness = true` for a baseline test, forcing the coarse-graining to be a declared, fixed property of the substrate, independent of the specific tally result [[20](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.04755)]. This mirrors the rigorous standards found in formal verification, where the properties to be verified must be clearly specified before the verification process begins [[24](https://www.math.pku.edu.cn/teachers/zhangnx/fm/materials/acm-onFM.pdf)]. The `CoarseGrainingSpec` thus serves as a powerful governance tool, preventing the "coarse outcome" from becoming a flexible parameter that can be adjusted to fit the desired narrative.

Finally, the `NullModelResult` is the framework's mechanism for adjudicating success. Its `status` field is not a binary pass/fail but a nuanced, consequence-driven classification. A status of `parity_null_survived` means the main model's behavior is fully replicated by the XOR automaton, triggering an automatic downgrade to `classical_parity_artifact`. A status of `survives_with_parity_debt_open` indicates the main model possesses additional, independently verified structure that the XOR null lacks, such as substrate-derived label origin or nontrivial coarse-graining independence [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. This sophisticated status system prevents ambiguous "partial passes" and forces a clear epistemic consequence for every null-model outcome, embodying the EBP v2.1 directive that "accounting must not become the work" [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. The debt ledger is not an end in itself but a precise map of what remains to be done.

## Null-Model Strategy: The Parity/XOR Automaton as the Primary Rival

The null-model strategy is the cornerstone of the framework’s epistemic rigor. Rather than seeking to prove a hypothesis, the Workbench is designed to stress-test it against increasingly sophisticated rivals, with the parity/XOR automaton serving as the primary, baseline null model. This choice is not arbitrary; it is grounded in the deep mathematical and computational understanding that XOR is a fundamental, ubiquitous, and highly expressive logical operation. XOR accepts two operands and compares their values; it evaluates to 0 (or False) if they are the same, and to 1 (or True) if they differ [[28](https://www.baeldung.com/cs/exclusive-or)]. Its reversibility makes it a natural candidate for a rival to a reversible automaton, as it is a classic example of a reversible logic gate, unlike AND or OR [[25](https://stackoverflow.com/questions/2326561/xor-reversibility-operation-question)]. This is why XOR is frequently used in encryption, particularly in Vernam ciphers, due to its inherent reversibility [[25](https://stackoverflow.com/questions/2326561/xor-reversibility-operation-question)].

The parity/XOR null model is constructed to replicate the `SignedTallyCancellationWitness` using only classical, non-quantum resources. Its core operation is to assign a `+1` or `-1` label to each history based solely on the *parity* of the number of transitions of a certain type along that history. For example, a simple parity rule might be: "assign `+1` if the history contains an even number of transitions from node A to node B, and `-1` otherwise." When two histories lead to the same coarse state, one with even parity and one with odd parity will contribute `+1` and `-1` respectively to the `SignedTally`, resulting in a cancellation of zero. This is mathematically identical to the outcome sought by the main model, but it is achieved through a mechanism that is demonstrably classical and local, with no appeal to superposition or global phase coherence.

The framework's strength lies in its explicit, automated pass/fail criteria for this null model. The default rule is unequivocal: *if the parity/XOR null reproduces the signed-tally cancellation under equivalent structure, the result is downgraded to `classical_parity_artifact`* [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. This is not a soft recommendation but a hard, automated downgrade. It means that the witness survives only as "toy-level signed-tally behavior explainable by classical parity," not as "interference-like structure." This automatic downgrade is the ultimate expression of the EBP v2.1 doctrine that "promotion costs debt." To avoid this downgrade, the main model must demonstrate *additional independent structure* that the XOR null lacks. This could include a label origin that is demonstrably derived from the substrate's geometry, a history dependence that is not reducible to a simple endpoint property, a composition behavior that shows scaling, or robustness across a family of related update rules [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. The burden of proof is placed squarely on the main model, not on the null model, reversing the typical burden of proof in physics and placing it on the side of the more complex, less parsimonious explanation.

Beyond the primary parity/XOR model, the framework mandates a comprehensive suite of eight null models to cover the full spectrum of potential "cheats" or trivial explanations. These include the `Constant` model (where the outcome is always the same), the `EndpointOnly` model (where the label depends only on the start and end points, not the path taken), the `Random` model (a stochastic baseline), and the `HandPainted` model (where labels are assigned arbitrarily to force the desired outcome) [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. Each of these models is not just a theoretical possibility but a concrete, executable specification. Their inclusion is informed by the broad landscape of formal verification, where benchmarks are drawn from diverse repositories covering compiler correctness, programming language semantics, and type theory [[4](https://arxiv.org/html/2602.18307v1)]. The `GenericPermutation` null model, for instance, tests whether the cancellation is a generic feature of any bijective update rule, while the `IdentityCoarse` model tests whether the coarse-graining map itself is the source of the effect [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)].

The `ForbiddenInferenceAudit` is the final, crucial component of the null-model strategy. It is a programmatically enforced constraint that prevents the automated reporting tool from generating text that would overclaim the significance of the result. Even if the `SignedTally` is zero, the audit object will contain a field like `forbidden_inferences: ["quantum_interference", "Born_rule", "Schrödinger_dynamics"]`, ensuring that the output report states only the formal, debt-bound conclusion: "A `SignedTallyCancellationWitness` was generated and confirmed to be non-explainable by the parity/XOR null model." This is a masterful application of the EBP principle that "no final-truth claim may be promoted" [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. It turns the prevention of overclaiming from a manual, subjective review into an automated, inescapable constraint.

The efficacy of this null-model strategy is demonstrated by its success in Phase 0a, where the triality-winding mechanism was subjected to a similar, rigorous test. A metric-matched generic rival was constructed and proven to lack nontrivial triality structure via a degree-profile obstruction. This rival then successfully supported the same kinematic and local-rule properties, forcing the downgrade of the uniqueness claim [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. This precedent proves that the null-model approach is not a theoretical exercise but a potent, working methodology for refining scientific claims. The parity/XOR model is simply the next, more fundamental, layer of this strategy, targeting the very concept of non-additivity at its most basic level.

## Debt Structure and Obstruction Ledger: Mapping the Path Forward

The debt structure of the framework is its most vital, living document. It is not a list of failures but a precise, prioritized map of the path forward, a dynamic ledger that tracks the evolution of the idea from a bare hypothesis to a physically grounded theory. The debts are categorized into several distinct, interlocking types, each representing a different kind of epistemic obligation. The `needMap` debt is perhaps the most profound, demanding a clear, explicit translation rule that bridges the gap between the reversible microstate dynamics and the effective complex information geometry that gives rise to phase-like structure [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. Without retiring this debt, the framework remains a study in combinatorics, not a bridge to quantum mechanics. The `needToyCheck` debt is the immediate, executable obligation, requiring the demonstration of actual interference in the minimal automaton, a task that must be carefully distinguished from classical probability mixing [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)].

A critical, newly identified debt is the `u1ExtensionDebt`, which addresses the "sign-smuggling" risk. The use of `+1` and `-1` is explicitly flagged as an open debt, acknowledging that while a two-label sign system can be embedded into the richer U(1) group, this embedding does not constitute a derivation of U(1) phase continuity, complex amplitudes, or Hilbert-space structure [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. This debt is a direct acknowledgment of the subtle line walked by the framework: it uses integer arithmetic for bookkeeping, but the framework's ultimate goal is to see if richer structures can be *derived* from this foundation, not smuggled in [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. The `needObstruction` debt, meanwhile, is dedicated to monitoring the "complex smuggling" obstruction, ensuring the toy doesn't secretly assume the answer it is trying to find [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)].

The obstruction ledger is a parallel, equally important document that records the known roadblocks and failure modes. One major obstruction is the "Quotient-Type Computability" issue, which arises when defining structures "up to relabeling." In constructive type theory, quotient types can severely hinder computability and `decide`-based toy checks in Lean, creating a disconnect between the Lean contract and the Go implementation [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. The recommended solution is to use canonical representatives (e.g., lexicographically sorted adjacency matrices) to avoid this debt, logging quotient types as a deferred obligation if needed. Another obstruction is the "XOR/Classical Cancellation Distinction," which asks how to rigorously distinguish a valid non-additive witness from a trivial XOR parity effect. The faithfulness review must verify that the `HistoryLabel` is genuinely path-dependent and not just an endpoint property, as the `EndpointOnly` null model would trivially pass if the label depended only on the final state [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)].

The `needFaithfulnessReview` debt is a direct legacy of the Phase 0a downgrade. It asks whether the mechanism lifts from the abstract triality-shadow graph to the actual D₄ root or 24-cell geometry. This is not a rescue mission but an open test. The debt remains visible, reminding us that the triality structure is a candidate witness, not a proven necessity [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. The `needQuantumBridge` debt is the most ambitious, encompassing the derivation or simulation of amplitude, interference, equivariant current, Schrödinger-like limits, and Born-rule behavior. This debt is vast and open, reflecting the immense gulf between a simple signed tally and a full quantum theory [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)].

The debt ledger is designed to be self-correcting. If a debt is incorrectly deemed "paid," the framework is equipped to detect it. For instance, if the `SignedTallyCancellationWitness` is generated but is subsequently shown to be perfectly replicable by the `Parity/XOR` null model, the `needToyCheck` debt is not retired; instead, the claim is downgraded, and the `parity_null_survived` debt is marked as paid, with a new `classical_parity_artifact` status [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. This is a textbook application of the EBP doctrine that "debt does not kill." The idea lives on, but its status is refined and its boundaries are sharpened. The ledger is not a scorecard for success or failure, but a dynamic, evolving record of the idea's current epistemic standing, its strengths, and its vulnerabilities.

## Strategic Roadmap: From TC-1B to Phase 0b and Beyond

The strategic roadmap for the Workbench is a carefully sequenced progression of artifacts, each designed to retire a specific slice of debt while respecting the strict hierarchical dependencies established by the EBP v2.1 protocol. The immediate next step is **TC-1B — First Go-Executable Toy Check Specification**. This artifact is the concrete instantiation of the abstract TC-1A contract. It will define a brutally minimal, executable example: a `MicroSpace` with 4 to 8 states, a specific nontrivial bijective `ReversibleUpdate` $U$, a fixed `CoarseGraining` map $\Pi$, and a declared `LabelRuleSpec`, such as `transitionParity` or `cycleParity`. Its sole job is to execute the `HistoryEnumerationSpec`, compute the `UnlabeledCount` and `SignedTally`, and produce output that perfectly matches the v0.1 Go report schema [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. Crucially, it must do this without introducing any new physics-facing language, adhering strictly to the defined contract.

Following TC-1B, the roadmap bifurcates into two parallel, complementary tracks: **Phase 0b Faithfulness Review** and **TC-2: Quantum Bridge Exploration**. Phase 0b is the direct continuation of the triadic mapping, asking whether the toy mechanism faithfully lifts from the abstract triality-shadow graph to the concrete D₄ root or 24-cell geometry. This phase is not a return to the Lisi-style trap of embedding particles into E₈; rather, it is a test of the geometric attractor hypothesis. It requires the construction of a `D4GeometryCandidate` and a `FaithfulShadowMap` that proves the sparse triality graph is a genuine, equivariant shadow of the deeper D₄ symmetry [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. The `D4RootCoordinateSpecV2.lean` file has already laid the groundwork by defining the 24 D₄ roots as explicit vectors in $\mathbb{Z}^4$, and `D4TrialityAutomorphism.lean` has provided the explicit, non-identity permutations for triality and reflection, proving their S₃ relations and adjacency preservation [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. The next step is `D4GeometryCandidateInstance.lean`, which will bundle these coordinate and automorphism definitions into the formal `D4GeometryCandidate` structure, paving the way for the `FaithfulShadowMap`.

Simultaneously, TC-2 represents a deliberate pivot towards the core physical recovery targets. It is here that the `needQuantumBridge` debt begins to be addressed. TC-2 will explore how the finite, discrete, reversible automaton can be used to derive or simulate the hallmarks of quantum mechanics. This will involve investigating the "equivariant quantum circuits / gauge-equivariant networks" as a modern mathematical precedent for enforcing symmetry without hardcoding it into the basement substrate [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. It will also draw on the "Information-Theoretic Renormalization Group (ITRG) / MERA" to address the `coarse_graining_map` debt, exploring how microstates flow to effective geometry [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. The goal is not to implement a full quantum algorithm, but to begin the systematic exploration of how features like amplitude, interference, and current emerge from the declared combinatorial rules.

The long-term vision for the Workbench extends far beyond Phase 0b and TC-2. The ultimate ambition is to reach a point where the `SignedTallyCancellationWitness` is not just a curious combinatorial artifact, but a recognized signature of a deeper, universal principle. This principle would be that non-additivity—the hallmark of quantum behavior—is not a primitive axiom of nature, but a *consequence* of a specific kind of relational structure guided by an optimal geometric attractor. The journey from a 4-state Go automaton to this grand vision is a marathon of debt retirement, not a sprint to a final truth. Each artifact, from `Basic.lean` to `D4GeometryCandidateInstance.lean`, is a small, concrete, and necessary step on that path. The framework's greatest strength is its unwavering commitment to this incremental, debt-driven process, a commitment that has already yielded its first major success: the Phase 0a downgrade, which stands as a gold standard for how null-model testing should function in this Workbench [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. The roadmap is not a promise of success, but a disciplined plan for honest, auditable inquiry.

## Critical Analysis: Hidden Assumptions and Overclaim Risks

Despite its rigorous design, the framework harbors several hidden assumptions that constitute its most significant vulnerabilities. The most prominent is the "global time smuggling" assumption. The `History` definition relies on `Nat.iterate R.U`, which presupposes a global, discrete, synchronous clock [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. This is a profound import, as it contradicts the "relational" premise of the substrate, which aims to derive spacetime and its symmetries from the network of relations, not to assume them as a background stage. This assumption is so deeply embedded in the definition of a history that it is easy to overlook, yet it is a fundamental departure from the spirit of theories like causal sets or spin foams, which strive for a truly background-independent formulation [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. Addressing this will require a radical rethinking of history enumeration, perhaps moving to a partial-order enumeration of events or a causal-set-inspired notion of "chronological depth" rather than step count.

A second, more insidious risk is the "sign-smuggling" assumption. While the framework correctly flags the use of `+1` and `-1` as debt, the very arithmetic operations performed on these labels are isomorphic to the real part of complex multiplication and addition [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. The `SignedTally` is computed as a sum: $$\text{SignedTally} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \text{WitnessLabel}_i$$ This summation is a continuous, linear operation that, in the limit of many histories, could inadvertently replicate the mathematics of path integrals or Feynman's checkerboard model, where amplitudes are summed over all possible paths [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. The framework's insistence on `ZMod 2` or `ZMod 3` for the `WitnessLabel` is a good first step, but it does not eliminate the risk. The danger is that the combinatorial structure of the automaton, when combined with this summation, will produce results that are *functionally equivalent* to quantum interference, without having derived the underlying quantum structure. This is the essence of the `u1ExtensionDebt`: the two-point sign system can be mapped to U(1), but that mapping does not constitute a derivation of the continuous, infinite-dimensional structure of the circle group [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)].

The third major overclaim risk is "retroactive justification." There is a high likelihood that the successful completion of Phase 0b—demonstrating that the triality-winding mechanism lifts to the D₄ root system—will be used to retroactively justify the Phase 0a triality necessity claim, despite the ledger explicitly forbidding it [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. The temptation to declare, "Well, now that we've shown it works for D₄, it must be necessary," is immense and runs counter to the EBP v2.1 doctrine. The ledger must be treated as a binding legal document, not a negotiable suggestion. The Phase 0b success would not resurrect the uniqueness claim; it would only show that the triality mechanism is *faithful* to a specific, rigid geometry. The "downgrade carryover" clause in the Phase 0b specification exists precisely to guard against this fallacy [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. It explicitly states that Phase 0b may test faithfulness but may not claim triality is necessary unless a new, stronger null-model test is passed [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)].

Another subtle risk is the "governance as completion" fallacy. The "TBA Hardened Rewrite" is imported as a "governance context / candidate framework / alive, not promoted" [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. Yet, there is a latent risk that the act of writing the TBA rewrite will be mistaken for the act of proving it. Governance is not a static, completed artifact; it is a living, debt-bearing process that must itself be stress-tested. The "TBA Hardened Rewrite" must be treated as a provisional, auditable contract, subject to the same null-model scrutiny as any other component. Its status as "alive, not promoted" is a permanent condition, not a temporary holding pattern.

Finally, the framework's reliance on a "brutal at the throne" approach carries the risk of creating a brittle, overly restrictive environment. The fear is that the relentless focus on debt payment and obstruction review will stifle creative intuition, turning the Workbench into a bureaucratic obstacle course. The resolution to this tension lies in the protocol itself. The "ideas enter free" principle ensures that imaginative exploratory work is always welcome. The "promotion costs debt" principle simply ensures that when that exploration is presented as a serious candidate, its obligations are made explicit and non-negotiable. The framework is designed to protect the search for truth from unearned certainty, not to prevent the search from happening. The Phase 0a downgrade was not a failure; it was the highest form of success, proving that the protocol worked exactly as intended to force a sharper, humbler claim boundary [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. The roadmap, therefore, is not a sequence of hurdles to be cleared, but a series of increasingly precise questions to be asked, with each answer refining the question for the next iteration.

## Conclusion: A Living Workbench for Humble Inquiry

In conclusion, the framework presented here is not a finished theory of everything, nor is it a guaranteed pathway to quantum gravity. It is, fundamentally, a *living Workbench*—a disciplined, debt-bearing instrument for conducting humble, rigorous inquiry into the origins of quantum structure. Its power lies not in what it asserts, but in what it forbids. It forbids the smuggling of complex numbers, U(1) phase, and Hilbert spaces. It forbids the conflation of a signed tally with a quantum amplitude. It forbids the promotion of a "triality witness" to a "triality necessity." And most importantly, it forbids the evasion of the null model, forcing the confrontation with the simplest, most parsimonious explanation for any observed non-additivity.

The successful execution of Phase 0a serves as the definitive proof-of-concept for this entire endeavor. The Workbench did not break when faced with a fair rival; it adapted, downgraded, and emerged with a more honest, more precise, and more useful understanding of the triality mechanism. It is now understood not as a unique, necessary structure, but as a "candidate witness for the toy kinematic mechanism" [[13](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353088178_The_Lean_4_Theorem_Prover_and_Programming_Language)]. This is the ultimate triumph of the EBP v2.1 protocol: it transformed a potential weakness into a strength, turning a failed uniqueness claim into a robust, falsifiable, and empirically grounded statement about the mechanism's capabilities and limitations.

The framework's future is not predetermined. It is a branching path, where each fork is determined by the outcome of the next executable test. Will the `SignedTallyCancellationWitness` survive the parity/XOR null model in TC-1B? If it fails, the framework will have conclusively shown that the observed non-additivity is a classical artifact, and the debt will shift to finding a richer, more discriminating label system. If it survives, the debt will shift to the `needQuantumBridge` and `needFaithfulnessReview` items, driving the work towards the D₄ geometry and the derivation of richer quantum-like phenomena. The Workbench is designed to be resilient to both outcomes, its value lying in its ability to generate clear, unambiguous answers, regardless of whether those answers confirm or challenge the investigator's initial intuition.

The ultimate lesson of this research is that the most valuable contribution a speculative physics project can make is not a grand, final theory, but a set of rigorous, reusable tools and protocols for distinguishing genuine emergence from clever artifact. The `SignedTallyCancellationWitness`, the `ForbiddenInferenceAudit`, the `HistoryEnumerationSpec`, and the `LabelRuleSpec` are all such tools. They are not the destination, but the compass and the sextant for the journey. They allow the Workbench to ask the right questions: not "Is this quantum?" but "What specific, testable, debt-bound conditions must be met for this to be considered a step toward quantum behavior?" This is the essence of the "Elephant Behind Physics" metaphor: the blind men, each grasping a different part of the elephant, were not wrong, but incomplete. The Workbench is the collective effort to map the elephant's shape, one rigorously tested, debt-retired, and null-model-audited feature at a time. The journey is the destination, and the ledger of debts is the map.
