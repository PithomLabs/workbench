# 2. Demote “one actual trajectory” from basement to emergent layer

Original ARHCD preserved Bohmian ontology by saying “only one trajectory is real.” 

That was useful philosophically, but dangerous physically. A single deterministic trajectory alone cannot produce interference. The later critique correctly argued that the actual trajectory should be demoted from the basement and made emergent from a richer state-space or amplitude structure. 

So the revised statement should be:

```text
Not:
  Reality is only one trajectory at the basement.

Instead:
  The basement is a relational amplitude/constraint structure.
  One actualized history or record emerges in the semiclassical limit.
```

This preserves the Bohmian intuition without collapsing into classical determinism.

New ARHCD actualization rule:

```text
There is one actualized effective history,
but it is guided or selected by a larger relational amplitude geometry.
```

So Bohmian mechanics becomes an **actualization layer**, not the fundamental substrate.

# 3. Replace “coarse-graining” with three distinct mechanisms

The biggest conceptual repair is to stop using “coarse-graining” as a magic word.

The critique correctly says ordinary coarse-graining throws away information and does not automatically generate quantum phase, interference, or unitary evolution. It proposes spectral mechanics, entanglement renormalization, and constraint dynamics as better mechanisms. 

So ARHCD v2 should distinguish:

```text
1. Spectral lifting
   reversible/discrete dynamics → operator spectrum → phase candidates

2. Entanglement renormalization
   quantum tensor network → scale-preserving emergence → geometry/QFT

3. Effective continuum coarse-graining
   microscopic/cutoff model → Navier–Stokes, GR, thermodynamics, etc.
```

Never call all three “coarse-graining.”

Use sharper language:

| Mechanism                    | What it does                                      | Risk                              |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Spectral lifting             | extracts phase from operator/eigenspace structure | may remain classical Koopman-like |
| Entanglement renormalization | preserves quantum information across scale        | may import quantum mechanics      |
| Effective continuum limit    | produces PDEs/fields from microstructure          | may lose cutoff-dependent effects |

This makes the framework much more honest.


# 5. Add a Domain-Wall / Bulk-Leakage layer

The domain-wall idea is imaginative and useful, but it changes the effective equations.

In ARHCD v2, we should encode it as:

```text
Domain-wall confinement:
  observed 3+1 physics is an effective boundary layer
  of a higher-dimensional relational/information geometry.

Bulk leakage:
  extreme localized coherence or curvature can activate hidden dissipative channels.
```

For Navier–Stokes, this becomes:

```text
∂ₜu + (u·∇)u = -∇p + νΔu + D_bulk[u]
```

Where `D_bulk[u]` is an effective high-energy dissipation term triggered only near extreme gradients/vorticity.

But the theory-debt ledger must say:

```text
D_bulk is speculative.
It is not part of classical Navier–Stokes.
It must vanish or become negligible in ordinary regimes.
It must not violate observed low-energy fluid behavior.
```

So the domain-wall hypothesis becomes a **high-energy completion** of continuum physics, not a proof of the continuum PDE.
