# Special Relativity's Ontological Overreach

### The experimental record confirms Lorentz covariance. It does not confirm that nature's deep structure lacks a preferred foliation.

---

The safe claim here is narrow and should be stated clearly at the outset: **special relativity is not false**. Its predictions are among the most precisely confirmed in all of physics — confirmed at the level of one part in 10¹⁸ in some atomic clock experiments, operationalized daily in GPS systems, encoded in the design of every particle collider on Earth. Nothing in what follows challenges that record.

The claim being examined is different, and more philosophical in character: that the *usual ontological reading* of special relativity — the claim that Lorentz invariance is a fundamental symmetry of nature's deep structure, not merely a symmetry of the observable surface — is epistemically stronger than what any experiment strictly establishes. Bohmian field theory serves as the diagnostic that makes this gap visible.

---

## What the Experiments Actually Establish

Special relativity rests on two postulates: the relativity principle (the laws of physics take the same form in all inertial frames) and the constancy of the speed of light. From these, Lorentz transformations follow, and with them time dilation, length contraction, the relativity of simultaneity, and the light-cone causal structure.

What the experimental record establishes is **Lorentz covariance of the observable algebra**: no measurement outcome distinguishes one inertial frame from another. The Michelson-Morley experiment, Kennedy-Thorndike, Ives-Stilwell, modern tests of CPT invariance and photon isotropy — all confirm that the observable predictions are frame-independent to extraordinary precision.

What the experiments do not establish — and cannot establish — is a claim about unobservable structure. The inference from "no experiment detects a preferred frame" to "no preferred frame exists" is not a logical consequence of the data. It is an additional metaphysical commitment, introduced by eliminating the undetectable as meaningless. That move, while methodologically powerful, is a philosophical choice rather than an experimental finding.

This distinction matters because it is precisely the territory where Bohmian field theory lives.

---

## Two Readings of Lorentz Invariance

It is useful to separate two interpretations that are often conflated.

**The Lorentz-Poincaré reading** (pre-Einstein, but technically equivalent to SR): there exists a preferred rest frame — originally conceived as the aether — but all moving rods and clocks are physically contracted and dilated in ways that make the frame empirically inaccessible. Lorentz covariance is a consequence of how matter behaves; it is not a primitive fact about spacetime geometry.

**The Einstein-Minkowski reading**: Minkowski spacetime is the fundamental arena. The Lorentz group is a symmetry of that arena's geometry. There is no preferred frame because spacetime itself has no such structure to give.

These two readings are **empirically equivalent** by construction. No experiment decides between them. The Einstein-Minkowski reading became dominant not because it was experimentally confirmed over its competitor, but because it was simpler, more elegant, and dissolved what looked like a suspicious conspiracy (the hiding of the aether frame). These are genuine virtues. They are not the same as experimental verification.

Harvey Brown's *Physical Relativity* (2005) makes this point rigorously: the Lorentz group can be interpreted dynamically — as a constraint on the behavior of matter in motion — rather than geometrically, as a primitive feature of spacetime. On the dynamical reading, SR tells you something about what matter does; it need not tell you that spacetime has no preferred structure. The difference is not idle philosophical hair-splitting. It has direct consequences for how one should read the tension between SR and quantum mechanics.

## Bohmian Field Theory as a Diagnostic

Bohmian mechanics assigns definite configurations to physical systems at all times, guided by a wave function (or wave functional, in the field-theoretic extension) that evolves according to the Schrödinger equation. The guiding equation is nonlocal: the velocity of any particle depends on the global wave function, not merely on local field values. This nonlocality is the price of determinism and definiteness; it is also, Bell showed, unavoidable for any theory that reproduces quantum mechanical predictions.

The relativistic extension — Bohmian field theory, in the tradition of Dürr, Goldstein, Norsen, and Zanghì — requires defining the guiding equation on a foliation of spacetime into spacelike hypersurfaces. To say what "the field configuration is now," across all of space, you need a notion of simultaneity. The equivariance theorem then guarantees that if the initial distribution of field configurations is given by the Born rule, it remains so under the Bohmian dynamics — and therefore no operational procedure can detect which foliation was chosen. The preferred frame exists in the ontology; it is invisible in the statistics.

This is not a defect specific to the Bohmian formulation. It is a symptom of a general fact: **Bell's theorem proves quantum nonlocality, and nonlocality requires some coordination structure that standard SR ontology does not supply**. The standard response is to say that quantum correlations simply do not require a mechanism — they just are. The Bohmian response is to say that if you want a mechanism, you need a foliation, and the price of having a foliation is committing to ontological structure that SR (on its usual reading) rules out.

Crucially, Bell's theorem rules out *local* hidden variable theories. A preferred foliation is a global, nonlocal structure. It is not ruled out by Bell. The usual claim that hidden variables are forbidden by Bell's theorem conflates local and nonlocal hidden variables — a conflation that Bohmian mechanics directly refutes.

## What the Equivariance Theorem Says and Does Not Say

The equivariance theorem is often taken to show that Bohmian field theory is "really" Lorentz invariant at the observable level, and therefore fine. This reading is correct but incomplete.

The theorem establishes that, under quantum equilibrium — meaning the distribution of actual field configurations is given by |Ψ|² — the observable predictions are Lorentz-covariant. This is a conditional result. Its condition is the Born-rule distribution holding universally.

The usual ontological reading of SR says: "No preferred frame." The equivariance theorem says: "No detectable preferred frame, conditioned on universal quantum equilibrium." These are different claims. The gap between them is not formal; it is the space where the preferred foliation lives, fully hidden, structurally real.

Whether that gap is troubling depends on what you think physics is for. If physics is the science of observable predictions, the gap is inconsequential. If physics is the science of what actually exists, the gap is the point.

## Emergent Lorentz Symmetry: Precedent Exists

The idea that a fundamental symmetry might be emergent rather than primitive has precedent throughout physics.

The most instructive example is condensed matter: graphene's low-energy effective theory is a (2+1)-dimensional Dirac equation with an effective "speed of light" (the Fermi velocity ≈ c/300). This effective Lorentz symmetry is exact at low energies and breaks down at the lattice scale — approximately 2.4 Å. The microscopic theory is not Lorentz invariant. The long-wavelength theory is.

The Wilsonian renormalization group provides the mechanism: Lorentz invariance can be an infrared fixed point even when the UV theory lacks it. All that is required is that the Lorentz-violating terms are irrelevant operators in the RG sense, flowing to zero at low energies.

Applied to fundamental physics: if the universe has a UV completion at or near the Planck scale that breaks Lorentz invariance, and if that breaking is by irrelevant operators from the low-energy perspective, then observed Lorentz invariance at accessible energies would be exact to any precision achievable, with no implication that the fundamental ontology is Lorentz-invariant. The Lorentz group would be a symmetry of the effective theory, not of nature.

This remains speculative as applied to fundamental physics. But it blocks the inference from "Lorentz symmetry is experimentally exact" to "Lorentz symmetry is ontologically fundamental." The argument from exact symmetry to fundamental status requires ruling out the emergent case — and that has not been done.

## The Valentini Seam: Where Ontology Becomes Empirical

The question whether the preferred foliation is merely metaphysical or potentially empirical turns on the status of quantum equilibrium.

In standard Bohmian mechanics, the Born-rule distribution is taken as an initial condition — a brute fact about the early universe. Antony Valentini has argued that this is too strong: the Born rule might instead be a *thermodynamic* equilibrium condition, analogous to thermal equilibrium in statistical mechanics. Just as thermal equilibrium is not a law of nature but a highly probable dynamical state, quantum equilibrium might be a likely but not necessary condition, reached from generic non-equilibrium initial conditions by a process Valentini calls "sub-quantum relaxation."

If quantum equilibrium is contingent rather than necessary, then non-equilibrium initial conditions could survive in some sector of the universe — most plausibly, in relics from before the equilibration time in the early universe. Such relics might appear in the cosmic microwave background as deviations from the Born-rule power spectrum, or as anomalous correlations in primordial fluctuations.

This is the seam where the ontological claim becomes testable. If quantum equilibrium always holds by necessity, the preferred foliation is permanently hidden — real in the ontology, silent in the phenomenology. If equilibrium is contingent and relaxation was incomplete, the foliation might leave a trace. Valentini and collaborators have developed this programme concretely. It is not established, but it is falsifiable — which is precisely the right epistemic status for a conjecture that has not been promoted.

## The Block Universe Problem: The Deeper Tension

The preferred foliation is not the deepest tension between SR and Bohmian field theory. That tension is the block universe.

The natural metaphysical reading of Minkowski spacetime — endorsed by Einstein himself in his correspondence and later by Weyl, Rindler, and many philosophers of physics — is the block universe: a static four-dimensional manifold in which all events exist equally. On this reading, the "flow" of time is an illusion; there is no ontologically privileged present; becoming is a feature of perspective, not of reality.

Bohmian mechanics is structurally committed to the opposite. The wave function evolves. The configuration of the world changes. There is a genuine distinction between past, actual present, and unactualized future. The theory is dynamical in a way that requires time to do real ontological work.

This tension is not merely about preferred frames. It is about whether time is a dimension of a static geometric object or a genuine parameter of evolution. The preferred foliation problem is downstream of this: you need a foliation *because* you are committed to a real "now," and the real "now" is in tension with the block universe reading of SR.

Tim Maudlin, in *Quantum Non-Locality and Relativity*, argues that this tension cannot be fully dissolved. At least one of the standard ontological readings must be revised: either the block universe reading of SR, or the dynamical, becoming-committed reading of quantum mechanics. There may be no position that preserves both fully intact.

The productive response is not to declare SR false, but to take seriously that SR's empirical content — Lorentz covariance of observables — and SR's usual metaphysical reading — the block universe with no preferred foliation — came packaged together for historical reasons, not logical ones. They can, in principle, be disentangled.

## The Disciplined Conclusion

The experimental record establishes, to extraordinary precision, that no operational procedure distinguishes one inertial frame from another. This will not change.

What the record does not establish is that this operational fact exhausts the ontological situation. The inference from "undetectable" to "nonexistent" is not entailed by the data; it is a methodological commitment — a powerful and productive one, but a commitment nonetheless.

Bohmian field theory is useful here not as a replacement for standard QFT, but as a diagnostic. It demonstrates that a theory can be operationally Lorentz-covariant while maintaining a preferred foliation in its ontology. The equivariance theorem makes this precise. The preferred foliation is hidden — but hidden by a theorem conditional on quantum equilibrium, not hidden by logical impossibility.

The safe claim is then: **the standard ontological reading of SR — that Lorentz invariance is a fundamental symmetry of nature's deep structure — is an epistemically stronger claim than what any experiment has established, or could establish within the quantum equilibrium regime**. That reading may be correct. It may be the simplest, most elegant, most parsimonious position. But it is a metaphysical posture, not an experimental finding.

Bohmian field theory does not prove it wrong. It proves it is a choice.

---

*The debt this argument carries, in honest accounting: it owes a map from Bohmian field ontology to the Lorentz-covariant observable algebra (partially paid by the equivariance theorem), a toy check distinguishing exact from approximate emergent Lorentz symmetry, a null-model comparison against retrocausal and many-worlds approaches that achieve Lorentz covariance without preferred foliations, and an obstruction assessment of whether any UV completion that is both Lorentz-violating and empirically adequate can be consistently defined. None of these debts kill the conjecture. They define the work still owed.*
