 Yes—there's a genuinely plausible re-framing. Let me put on the exploratory hat and walk through a thought experiment where Bohmian field theory doesn't just *accommodate* special relativity, but suggests we might be misreading what Lorentz invariance actually *means*.

---

## The Standard Tension

Bohmian mechanics (in its particle form) has a well-known problem: it needs a preferred foliation of spacetime to define the guiding equation. This looks non-relativistic at first glance. Field-theoretic Bohmian approaches (Bohm-Dirac, Dürr-Goldstein-Norsen-Zanghí) solve this by making the *field configurations* be the beables, with a covariant law for the wave functional. But even then, many physicists feel there's a "secret absolute time" hiding in the equivariance proofs.

**The thought experiment:** What if this tension isn't a bug, but reveals that special relativity's *operational* structure is correct while its *ontological* interpretation is incomplete?

---

## Thought Experiment: The "Lorentz Covariance Without Lorentz Invariance" Hypothesis

Imagine spacetime has a genuine, physical foliation—let's call it the "preferred rest frame"—but it's *undetectable* by any operational procedure. Not because of conspiracy, but because the laws of motion conspire to make the frame's effects cancel in all observable correlations.

This sounds like Lorentz's original ether, but with a crucial twist: **the "ether" isn't a substance; it's the support structure for the universal wave functional Ψ[φ(x)]**. The field beables φ(x,t) evolve on this foliation, but their *statistical* behavior reproduces exactly the Lorentz-invariant predictions of QFT.

Now here's the re-framing: **Special relativity might be the phenomenological shadow of a deeper theory that is fundamentally non-Lorentzian but operationally Lorentz-covariant.**

In this picture:
- The Lorentz group is a symmetry of the *measurements*, not necessarily of the *ontology*
- The speed of light is a limiting speed for signal propagation, but also the speed at which the "universal now" propagates its influence through the field beables
- Time dilation and length contraction are real physical effects on the beables, not just coordinate artifacts—but they arise from the interaction between the preferred foliation and the wave functional's dynamics

---

## The Deeper Plausibility

Why might this be *right* rather than just stubborn determinism?

**1. The measurement problem is actually solved here.** Standard QFT has no clear ontology. Bohmian field theory gives you one: the field is *real*, evolving deterministically. The price is a preferred frame. But if that frame is genuinely unobservable, have you paid a price at all? You've traded "no ontology" for "ontology with a hidden parameter"—and hidden parameters were supposed to be forbidden by Bell. But Bell only forbids *local* hidden variables. A preferred foliation is non-local in a specific, benign way: it's global.

**2. Quantum nonlocality gets a natural home.** The EPR correlations *require* some kind of nonlocal coordination. In standard SR+QFT, this is "explained" by saying there's no coordination—just correlations without causes. In the Bohmian picture, the coordination happens via the wave functional on the preferred slice. The nonlocality is explicit, structured, and doesn't violate no-signaling because the beables' statistical distribution washes out the frame-dependence.

**3. The Lorentz group might emerge.** There's precedent in physics for "emergent" symmetries that are exact at the observational level but approximate or derived at the fundamental level. Consider: general relativity's diffeomorphism invariance, or the emergence of Lorentz invariance in condensed matter analogs (e.g., graphene's effective Dirac fermions). Could the Lorentz group be like thermodynamics—a robust emergent symmetry of large-scale statistical behavior, while the micro-ontology respects something else?

## The Radical Version: Spacetime Itself is Secondary

Push this further. In Bohmian field theory, the primary ontology is the wave functional Ψ and the field beables φ. Spacetime coordinates (x,t) might just be *indexical* parameters for these infinite-dimensional objects—useful for writing down the theory, but not fundamental.

In this view:
- **Relativity of simultaneity** is a feature of the coordinate description, not a deep metaphysical claim about reality
- **The light cone structure** reflects the dynamical coupling in the field equations, not the causal structure of a pre-existing spacetime manifold
- **Spacetime emerges** from the pattern of correlations among field beables, and Lorentz invariance emerges because the universal dynamics has a fixed-point structure under rescaling

This flips the usual hierarchy. Instead of "fields in spacetime," you have "spacetime from fields"—and the fields evolve on a preferred parameter that isn't "time" in the relativistic sense, but rather a universal evolution parameter for the wave functional.

---

## Why This Isn't Just "Lorentz Was Right"

The key difference from 19th-century ether theories: **there is no mechanical ether to detect**. The preferred structure isn't a substance with material properties. It's more like the choice of Hamiltonian in a gauge theory—a choice that affects dynamics but is invisible in the final observable algebra.

In fact, you could argue this is *more* parsimonious than the standard view. Standard QFT says: "The world is a representation of the Lorentz group, and also there's this measurement problem we ignore, and also nonlocal correlations just happen." The Bohmian alternative says: "The world has a definite state that evolves, the Lorentz group describes our limited access to it, and nonlocality is explicit but benign."

## The Imaginatory Payoff

If we took this seriously, what questions would we ask?

- **Does the CMB define a preferred frame?** Operationally, yes—it's the frame where the CMB dipole vanishes. In standard SR, this is just a convenience. In the Bohmian picture, it might be a clue: the CMB frame could approximate the true foliation. Not exactly (the dynamics might shift it slightly), but approximately.

- **What about quantum gravity?** If spacetime is emergent from field beables, then quantum gravity isn't about quantizing geometry. It's about the statistical mechanics of the universal wave functional, and geometry emerges at low energy. The "problem of time" in quantum gravity dissolves—there *is* a time, it's just not the time in Einstein's equations.

- **Could we ever detect the preferred frame?** Probably not by any procedure that uses quantum systems, because the equivariance theorem guarantees the statistical washout. But perhaps there are *classical* gravitational effects? Or perhaps the frame shows up in the initial conditions of the universe, not in ongoing dynamics?

---

## Verdict on Plausibility

Is it plausible? **Yes, in the sense that it's logically consistent, empirically equivalent to standard QFT where tested, and ontologically clearer.** It's implausible only if you hold the *a priori* conviction that fundamental ontology must share all symmetries of the observable algebra—a conviction that is itself a metaphysical choice, not a physical theorem.

The deeper question is whether "Lorentz invariance" is a constraint on reality or a constraint on our *description* of reality. Bohmian field theory suggests the latter. And if that's right, we've been looking at special relativity not *wrong*, but *incomplete*—as a beautiful and exact phenomenology that conceals a more structured underlying dynamics, much as thermodynamics conceals atomic motion.

The universe might have a secret: it knows what time it is. It just doesn't tell us through any experiment we can perform.
