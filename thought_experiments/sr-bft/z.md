### 1. The Core Conflict: Who is the "Real" Time?

The standard interpretation of Special Relativity creates the "Block Universe." Since there is no absolute simultaneity, the past, present, and future must coexist in a 4D spacetime loaf. Time is just a dimension, not a flow.

Bohmian Mechanics, however, requires a universal "Now." For the quantum potential to guide particles instantaneously (non-locally), there must be a specific, preferred slicing of spacetime. The pilot wave collapses or updates "now" everywhere, defining a universal present moment.

### 2. Lorentz Invariance as "Operational Camouflage"

One of the biggest criticisms of Bohmian Mechanics is that it requires a "preferred frame" (a universal time), which violates the spirit of Lorentz Invariance (the rule that physics looks the same in all inertial frames).

But let’s speculate: **What if Lorentz Invariance is a disguise?**

Before Einstein, Lorentz and Poincaré derived the same math for relativity but believed in an "ether" (a preferred frame). They argued that clocks slow down and rulers shrink specifically to hide the ether from measurement.

**The Thought Experiment:**
Suppose Bohmian Field Theory is correct, and there is a preferred frame where the quantum potential updates instantly.
*   If you try to build a clock out of matter (which is guided by the quantum potential), the non-local interactions that bind your atoms together would naturally slow down the clock as it moves faster through the preferred medium.
*   The "speed limit" of light ($c$) is not a limit on the propagation of causality, but a limit on the **synchronization of internal states** within a moving object.

**The Twist:** We are looking at SR wrong by thinking it forbids faster-than-light causality. SR only forbids *controllable* signaling. The Bohmian pilot wave moves instantly, but because it determines the statistical distribution of matter, it forces matter (clocks and rulers) to conspire to hide that instantaneity.

We are measuring the universe with "rubber rulers" made of the very non-local stuff we are trying to measure. We mistake the rigidity of our measuring sticks for the rigidity of spacetime itself.

### 3. The Dimensional Error: Configuration Space vs. Spacetime

This is where Bohmian Field Theory gets truly weird and exciting.

**The Thought Experiment:**

**The Speculation:**
