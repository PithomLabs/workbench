# Coherence & Gap Analysis — bohmian.md vs. Copenhagen + Beyond

**Method:** Each prompt/answer pair is evaluated for (a) internal coherence, (b) fidelity to standard Copenhagen QM claims, (c) gaps or limitations, and (d) where input from Loop Quantum Gravity (LQG), String Theory, or other frameworks would strengthen or challenge the argument.

**Legend:**
- ✅ = Coherent / Well-supported
- ⚠️ = Partially coherent / Needs qualification
- ❌ = Significant gap or misrepresentation
- 🔗 = Where LQG/String/Other could help

# Summary: Cross-Cutting Themes

## 1. Copenhagen Misrepresentation
Multiple pairs (02, 19, 24) caricature Copenhagen as "observer-dependent" or "instrumental" in a pejorative sense. Copenhagen is an epistemic interpretation, not a claim that reality depends on humans. This straw man weakens the comparative argument.

## 2. Quantum Gravity Absence
The document discusses Bohmian mechanics in cosmological contexts (Big Bang, quantum gravity) without engaging with actual quantum gravity frameworks (LQG, string theory, asymptotic safety). The "bounce," "Problem of Time," and "emergent spacetime" claims are made in a vacuum, without comparison to established results in these fields.

## 3. Quantitative Deficit
Nearly all claims are qualitative ("solves," "proves," "dissolves"). Where quantitative predictions exist (e.g., Valentini's CMB power deficit), they are not presented. This makes the claims difficult to falsify or compare with experiment.

## 4. Internal Inconsistency
Pair 05 assumes quantum equilibrium to explain homogeneity, while Pair 07 assumes non-equilibrium to explain anomalies. These are mutually exclusive starting conditions. A phase transition story is needed but absent.

## 5. Lorentz Invariance Oversimplification
The document presents Lorentz invariance as a major problem for Bohmian mechanics (Pairs 18, 21, 22) but omits Tumulka's flash QFT, which achieves Lorentz invariance without a preferred frame. This omission makes the "problem" appear more severe than it is.

## 6. Ontological Assumption
The document assumes realism (beables, substrates) is mandatory and Copenhagen is deficient for lacking it. But anti-realist interpretations (Copenhagen, QBism, relational QM) reject this premise. The argument is question-begging for readers who do not share the realist commitment.
