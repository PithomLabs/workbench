Worth pulling on — but let me be precise about what's being proposed, because there's a version of this that's a category error and a version that's actually interesting.

**What holography is actually a claim about**

The holographic principle isn't really a claim about *where information is written* in some loose sense. It's a claim about *degrees-of-freedom counting*: the maximum entropy (≈ log of the number of distinguishable states) obtainable in a spatial region scales with the boundary area in Planck units, not the volume. That's the Bekenstein-Hawking result (S = A/4G for black holes), generalized by 't Hooft and Susskind, and it's not a free-floating postulate — it's forced on you by black hole thermodynamics plus the generalized second law, and it's *realized concretely* in AdS/CFT, where a (d+1)-dimensional bulk gravity theory turns out to have exactly the same Hilbert space as a d-dimensional boundary CFT (Maldacena; sharpened via Ryu-Takayanagi's entanglement-entropy = minimal-surface-area formula).

**Why "info lives in configuration space" doesn't, by itself, touch this**

Here's the trap: Bohmian field configuration space for a field theory scales with the *volume* of degrees of freedom — every point in space contributes a field-value coordinate. So naively, "information is encoded in configuration space" gives you a volume law, not an area law. You haven't dissolved the puzzle, you've reproduced the naive EFT expectation that holography itself overturns. Worse: since standard Bohmian mechanics is empirically equivalent to orthodox QM on the *same* Hilbert space — trajectories are extra structure riding on top of Ψ, not a replacement for it — any entanglement-entropy area-law that's already present in Ψ (the free-field vacuum, say) is exactly as present whether or not you add Bohmian beables. You can't get a *different* answer to "how much info fits in a region" just by adding trajectories, because that answer is a property of Ψ, which both formulations share. So "no need for holography" is too strong a claim as stated — it would require BFT to predict something orthodox QFT+gravity doesn't, and that's a much bigger ask than relocating an interpretation.

**Where the actually interesting version lives**

The move that *is* available to you is the one Bohmian mechanics always makes: not eliminating a phenomenon, but converting a postulate into something derived from deeper dynamics — the way it explains Born-rule statistics via quantum equilibrium instead of positing |ψ|² as axiomatic.

And you've already built the right scaffolding for this without realizing it's adjacent to holography: BFT's grounding in **Haag's theorem and the Unruh effect** is the *same* machinery — vacuum entanglement, modular theory, Bisognano-Wichmann — that independently underwrites the major "holography as derived thermodynamics" results: Jacobson's 1995 derivation of the Einstein equation from the Clausius relation applied to local Rindler horizons (using exactly the Unruh temperature you're already leaning on), and the later entanglement-entropy-first-law derivations of linearized Einstein equations (Faulkner, Guica, Hartman, Myers, Van Raamsdonk). The area law for entanglement entropy in the free-field vacuum itself goes back to Bombelli-Koch-Lee-Sorkin and Srednicki — it's a short-distance/UV property of the vacuum state restricted to a subregion, not something tied to any interpretation.

So the defensible, BFT-flavored claim isn't "configuration space replaces the holographic principle." It's closer to: *the same preferred-foliation, field-beable ontology you're using to ground the Unruh effect and dissolve the problem of time might also supply the missing ontic mechanism underneath the entanglement-thermodynamics derivations of holography* — i.e., BFT as a hidden-variables completion of *why* the modular/entanglement structure has the form it does, rather than a competitor that makes the area law optional.

**A toy check that would actually test something**

If you wanted a first finite check rather than just a narrative: take the free-field vacuum restricted to a Rindler wedge (or a flat boundary half-space), where the orthodox entanglement entropy area-law is already computed (Srednicki). Compute the Bohmian conditional-wave-function / reduced-density-matrix structure for the same state, and ask whether anything in the preferred-foliation trajectory ensemble picks out a *distinguished* derivation of that same area scaling — versus just inheriting it passively from Ψ with the foliation doing no work. If the foliation is doing no work, that's an honest `needObstruction` retirement (idea downgraded to "compatible re-narration," not new physics). If it *does* constrain something — e.g. predicts foliation-dependent corrections to the area law — that's a genuinely novel, falsifiable claim worth chasing.

I'd frame the claim for now as "compatibility/explanatory worth exploring," not uniqueness — the null-model question (does anything besides BFT already explain area-law entanglement this way? yes, Jacobson/FGHMVR do it without any hidden-variable ontology at all) is the sharpest open debt here.
