## prompt

is shape dynamics agnostic to bohmian mechanics and other interpretations of qm

answer:

Shape Dynamics is fundamentally agnostic to interpretations of quantum mechanics because it is a classical theory of gravity and space, not a quantum theory. It reformulates General Relativity by replacing spacetime reframing symmetries with spatial scale (conformal) invariance. [1, 2, 3, 4, 5] 
However, when physicists attempt to quantize Shape Dynamics or apply its relational principles to quantum mechanics, it is uniquely sympathetic—not completely indifferent—to Bohmian mechanics. [6, 7] 
The structural relationship between Shape Dynamics and various quantum interpretations breaks down into several key areas:
## 1. The Natural Synergy with Bohmian Mechanics
While Shape Dynamics itself does not enforce Bohmian mechanics, it shares an exceptionally strong conceptual and mathematical bond with it. [6] 

* Shared Configuration Space: Both theories are formulated on the "configuration space" of the entire universe. Shape Dynamics defines physics on a "shape space" where only the relative angles and ratios between particles matter.
* Elimination of the Relativistic Time Problem: Standard Bohmian mechanics requires a preferred, absolute time slicing to define its non-local "guiding equation". Standard General Relativity forbids this. Shape Dynamics naturally provides exactly what Bohmian mechanics needs: a preferred spatial slicing (Constant Mean Curvature) where local scale is a gauge, but a universal cosmic time emerges.
* The "Pure Shape" Bohmian Models: Theoretical physicists have successfully constructed exact models of [Pure Shape Dynamics using the de Broglie–Bohm framework](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10701-024-00776-4). In these models, the universe performs a deterministic trajectory through a timeless shape space, guided by a universal wave function.

## 2. Compatibility with Other Interpretations
Because Shape Dynamics yields the same classical predictions as General Relativity, it can technically serve as the gravitational background for other interpretations, though with varying degrees of conceptual friction: [2, 8] 

| Quantum Interpretation | Compatibility with Shape Dynamics | Conceptual Friction / Synergy |
|---|---|---|
| Bohmian Mechanics (Pilot Wave) | High Synergy | Highly compatible. Shape Dynamics resolves the conflict between Bohmian non-locality and relativistic spacetime by providing a preferred frame. |
| Many-Worlds (Everett) | Possible | Achievable if the universal wave function branches into multiple historical trajectories across the timeless "shape space" landscape. |
| Copenhagen Interpretation | Low / Poor | Highly uncomfortable. Copenhagen relies on an external, classical "observer" or macroscopic measurement apparatus. Shape Dynamics is strictly a global, Machian framework meant to describe the entire closed universe relationally from within. |
| Relational Quantum Mechanics (RQM) | Moderate | Strong philosophical alignment. Both frameworks insist that absolute quantities (like absolute position or size) do not exist, and only relative relationships are real. |

## Summary
If you are treating Shape Dynamics purely as a classical alternative to General Relativity, it remains completely agnostic to quantum interpretations. But if your goal is to build a complete, relational quantum theory of the universe, Shape Dynamics actively paves a much smoother path for Bohmian mechanics than it does for orthodox, observer-dependent alternatives.
