**Start with what each theory actually aches for**

Bohmian mechanics aches for *reality*. It insists particles are somewhere, always — a definite configuration q(t) in physical space, whether or not anyone is watching. The wave function Ψ is real, and it *guides* those particles via the guidance equation. What Bohmian mechanics cannot stand is the measurement postulate, the observer-dependence, the collapse as magic trick.

Objective collapse theory aches for *finality*. It insists that the other branches — the ones not realized — must actually go away. Not just become inaccessible, not just decohere into effective invisibility, but physically cease to be. The superposition genuinely ends. What OCT cannot stand is the proliferation of empty branches that Bohmian mechanics happily tolerates.

They seem contradictory. Bohmian mechanics says the wave function never collapses. OCT says it must. But notice what each theory is *not* saying: Bohmian mechanics says the *particle* was never in superposition. OCT says the *wave function* collapses. These are two different things.

**The dissolving contradiction**

In Bohmian mechanics, after a measurement-like interaction, the particle is already *in* one branch. The guidance equation steered it there. The other branches — the empty waves, as they're called — still exist in Ψ, still evolve by Schrödinger, but they no longer influence the particle because they've separated in configuration space.

So ask: what if objective collapse is precisely the physical process that *eliminates those empty waves*? Not changing where the particle is — it was already somewhere — but removing from physical reality the branches that carry no particle.

That's the synthesis move. Collapse doesn't choose the outcome. The particle's actual position already determined the outcome. Collapse just cleans up. The apparent randomness of OCT is not fundamental randomness — it's our ignorance of q(t), the hidden variable. Given q(t), collapse is deterministic. But q(t) is inaccessible to us, so collapse *appears* stochastic. This dissolves the deepest apparent contradiction between the two frameworks.

**The framework: Guided Collapse Ontology**

Three primitive ontological elements. Nothing else is fundamental.

**I.** The pilot field Ψ — a real wave functional on configuration space Q. It carries causal structure, evolves by the Schrödinger equation between collapses, and guides particle motion.

**II.** The particle configuration q(t) — actual positions of all particles at all times. Definite. Always. This is Bohmian.

**III.** Spacetime geometry g_μν — dynamical, curved, real. But crucially: *sourced by the actual q(t)*, not by the quantum expectation value ⟨T_μν⟩. Einstein's equations read: G_μν = 8πG T_μν\[q(t)\]. Gravity responds to where things actually are, not to the smeared quantum average.The four arrows are the whole dynamics in miniature. Ψ guides q(t). The superposition of spacetime geometries in Ψ accumulates gravitational self-energy E_G. When E_G exceeds ℏ/τ (the Penrose threshold), collapse fires. q(t) — which has been sitting in one branch all along — determines which branch survives. The empty branches are pruned from Ψ. The cycle restarts.

**Recasting quantum mechanics**

The measurement postulate vanishes. There are no measurements, only interactions. When q(t) couples to a macroscopic pointer variable, the pointer's own mass makes E_G shoot past the threshold almost immediately — Penrose collapse fires, empty branches disappear, the "reading" is recorded. The appearance of wave function collapse during measurement is just this threshold being crossed rapidly for heavy, macroscopic systems.

The Born rule is not a postulate. It's an equilibrium condition. Particles distributed according to |Ψ|² is the unique stable distribution under the Bohmian guidance dynamics — quantum equilibrium, already proven within BM. In GCO it carries over unchanged.

The Schrödinger equation is exact — between collapses. During collapse, Ψ is projected onto the branch containing q(t). The Schrödinger equation gets modified only at collapse events, which for microscopic systems are extremely rare (E_G accumulates slowly for lightweight superpositions).

Superposition is real. It's not a state of ignorance — Ψ genuinely has multiple branches. But the particle is always in exactly one of them. The other branches are physically real but causally inert with respect to q(t) once they've separated. Penrose collapse periodically eliminates them, keeping the theory from accumulating an infinite graveyard of empty waves.

**Recasting general relativity**

The standard semiclassical approach sources Einstein's equations with ⟨T_μν⟩ — the expectation value of stress-energy. This is known to be inconsistent in regimes where Ψ has significant support in multiple macroscopically distinct configurations. GCO replaces this with T_μν\[q(t)\]: the stress-energy tensor of the actual particle configuration.

This makes gravity *classical at the ontological level*. Spacetime responds to where things are, not to quantum superpositions of where things might be. The apparent quantumness of gravity — the superposed spacetime geometries that make Penrose collapse happen — is entirely in Ψ, not in g_μν itself.

The preferred foliation problem that haunts Bohmian mechanics gets a Machian resolution. The preferred time slicing is not a fixed background structure imposed by hand — it's dynamically determined by the matter distribution q(t). The dominant mass-energy configuration of the universe at any moment defines the preferred foliation. At cosmological scales this coincides with the FLRW time coordinate and the CMB rest frame. The preferred frame is not arbitrary; it's the universe's own center-of-mass frame, selected by matter itself.

Near black hole singularities, the density of q(t) configurations becomes extreme, driving E_G to very high values and causing Penrose collapse to fire continuously. This creates a self-regulating mechanism: the wave function is perpetually localized near the singularity, which may prevent q(t) from ever actually reaching the classical singularity — the guidance equation cannot steer a particle to a configuration the wave function hasn't reached.

Information is never lost. Hawking radiation appears thermal because the wave function of radiation pairs looks thermal when the Bohmian correlations are traced out. But q(t) evolves continuously and reversibly (the guidance equation is deterministic). The Bohmian trajectories of in-falling matter and outgoing Hawking pairs carry the information in their correlations. An observer outside cannot access it, but it exists.

**Novel predictions**

The framework makes at least three predictions that differ from standard QM:

One: collapse rate depends on mass and scale. Large superpositions collapse faster. This is the Penrose prediction, but GCO sharpens it — the collapse outcome is determined by q(t), so interferometry experiments on massive objects should see not just decoherence but actual position definite collapse, with statistics consistent with |Ψ|² at quantum equilibrium. This is testable with near-future space interferometry (MAQRO-class experiments).

Two: the preferred foliation coincides with the CMB rest frame. This makes a prediction: Bell-inequality violations measured in different reference frames should show a very slight asymmetry, with the CMB rest frame being the one where the correlations appear most symmetric. The signal is tiny but in principle measurable.

Three: the MIPT critical point has a gravitational interpretation. In quantum circuits with measurements, the phase transition between volume-law and area-law entanglement corresponds in GCO to the Penrose threshold — the measurement rate at which E_G per gate exceeds ℏ/τ. This gives a parameter-free prediction for where the MIPT critical point should sit, relating it to the gravitational self-energy of the qubit superpositions involved. A Bohmian field theory on a lattice should allow a direct numerical test of whether the Bell jump rate in a finite Kitaev chain shifts relative to the known MIPT critical exponents when gravitational self-energy corrections are included.
